<div id="outline-container-5" class="outline-3"> <h3 id="sec-5">Emphasis and monospace</h3> <div class="outline-text-3" id="text-5">

<p></p> <p> You can make words <b>bold</b>, <i>italic</i>, <span style="text-decoration:underline;">underlined</span>, <code>code</code> and <code>verbatim</code>, and, if you must, <code>+strike-through+</code>. Text in the code and verbatim string is not processed for Org-mode specific syntax; it is exported verbatim. </p> <p> </p></div>

</div>

<div id="outline-container-6" class="outline-3"> <h3 id="sec-6">Horizontal rules</h3> <div class="outline-text-3" id="text-6">

<p></p> <p> A line consisting of only dashes, and at least 5 of them, will be exported as a horizontal line (<code>&amp;lt;hr/&gt;</code> in HTML and <code>\hrule</code> in LaTeX). </p> <p>

</p> <hr/> <p> </p> <p> As shown previously. </p> <p> </p></div>

</div>

<div id="outline-container-7" class="outline-3"> <h3 id="sec-7">Comment lines</h3> <div class="outline-text-3" id="text-7">

<p></p> <p> Lines starting with <code>#</code> in column zero are treated as comments and will never be exported. If you want an indented line to be treated as a comment, start it with <code>#+ =. Also, entire subtrees starting with the word =COMMENT</code> will never be exported. Finally, regions surrounded by <code>#+BEGIN_COMMENT</code> &hellip; <code>#+END_COMMENT</code> will not be exported. </p> <p> </p> <p> </p> <p> </p></div>

</div>
